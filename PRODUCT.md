# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two overlapping audiences: existing regulars who trust the business and want to book fast without friction, and new clients discovering Sassy Lash & Skin who need to feel confident and a little excited before committing. Both land on the same booking page. Regulars need clarity; new clients need warmth. The design must serve both without prioritizing either.

## Product Purpose

Online appointment booking for Sassy Lash & Skin, a one-person eyelash extension studio. Replaces the manual text-the-owner workflow. Clients browse services, pick a time, submit their info, and wait for SMS confirmation. The owner confirms or denies via SMS or admin dashboard. Success: a client completes a booking feeling like they just walked into the studio, not like they filed a form.

## Brand Personality

Soft, feminine, luxe. Like a well-designed local boutique — personal and warm, not corporate. Feels handcrafted. The studio has a name with personality in it (Sassy) and the design should honor that without leaning into cliché.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS booking tools: Calendly, Acuity — corporate blue, grid tables, zero personality. This should feel like the opposite.
- Dark or edgy aesthetics: not goth, not high-contrast dark, not moody editorial.
- Cheap feminine: pink glitter, everything-in-script-font, clip-art florals, feels low-budget. Luxe, not dollar-store pink.

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth over efficiency.** Every step in the booking flow — picking a service, choosing a time, filling the form — should feel like walking into the studio, not using software.
2. **Elevated personal touch.** The design should signal a real human behind it, not a template. Specific over generic. Considered over convenient.
3. **Soft confidence.** Feminine without being delicate. Luxe without being cold or intimidating. The brand has a name with a personality — let it show.
4. **Quality signals quality.** The care in the visual design implies the care in the service. Rough edges, generic choices, or obvious defaults undermine that.
5. **Clean steps, no friction theater.** The booking is the point. Don't decorate obstacles. Simplicity in the flow, richness in the feel.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort. Good contrast and readability as defaults. No specific WCAG audit required, but text should be legible, interactive elements clearly distinguishable, and the flow usable without color alone.
